Subtlety Guide

Basics

The purpose of this guide is to communicate my thesis and evaluations aswell as provide basic knowlage of the class and specification. I will try to cover the majority of questions and go into detail. If there are still questions left feel freee to contact me.

Introduction:

Subtlety got a lot of changes in 7.2.5 witch opened up a high range of talent choices that impact our cooldown management, playstyle and damage distribution.

The current iteration provides you with regular high burst phases, strong mobility and extremely good soaking capacity.
In addition to that Subtlety becomes the best class to bring higher Single target damage in AoE situations. One of the downsides of the spec is the high amount of bugs we have and partly abuse to get stronger damage output.
What we dont provide is strong execute or downtime damage or extreme AoE, but there are other classes that fit this categorie.

Attributes:

Attributes are the base values for our character's combat ability.
We have primary and secondary Attributes. Our secondary attributes are Agility and Starmina and out secondary attributes are Critical Strike Rating, Haste, Mastery and Versatility.

These Stats grow with your character level and are present on equipment we wear and impact our combat behaviour to a certain degree. Most gear pieces offer both primary and 2-3 secoundary Attributes and scale with the Item Level of the Gear piece.
Exceptions are Juwlry that offers only Starmina as main stat.

The Idea is to wear gear that offers the more beneficial attributes while still having an overall balanced distribution.
Gear Pieces that dont offer Agility are more dependent on the secoundary attributes and it can happen that a item that has a lower item level offers more benefits then a higher one. Gear pieces with Agility tent to have a high impact from the primary attribute and will often provide benefits even if they offer sub optimal secoundary attributes.
The amount you get from 1 point into one secoundary attribute depends on the attrubut.

Position, Range and Hitbox:

Every ability from your Spellbook has a certain range bound to it. This number represents the maximum distance you can stand relative to your target while beeing able to peform the action. Rogue is a close range damage dealing class and therefore the majority abilities are set to a range of 5 called melee range.The hitbox is an additional proporty of Enemys that can change this value to account for its size allowing us to stand abit further away.

In addition to that we have Dodge, Parry and Block.Creatures can not parry attacks but can dodge attacks from behind.
In addition to that we have an combo point generating ability that we use quite frequently that deals additional damage if we are behind the enemy calles [Backstab] (if not talented in [Gloomblade]).

The Gameplay revolves around staying behind the target the majority of the time while beeing within melee range to be able to peform abilities and automated attacks.

Behind the target doesent nessecery means visualy behind.The game counts angles that are more to the side as behind the target.

Ressources:

Our main ressource is Energy. The character has a base amount of 100 energy that regenerates with 10 energy/secound. The regeneration of this ressource can be higher with procs sutch as [Energetic Stabbing]or [Shadow Techniques]and with points in the secoundary attribute haste by the rate of 1/sec with every 10% haste rating.
The Amount can be incresed with the [Vigor]Talent, Items sutch as [Stabilized Energy Pendant]or the race recail from gnome [Expansive Mind].

Energy can be spend to create combo points that can be spend to use finishing moves or provide soaking/heal utility with abilities like [Feint].

Abilities:

Talents are the base that defines our gameplay. They come in diffrent categories and with diffrent conditions.
Passive abilities and Cooldowns enhance our gameplay by providing additional ressources, damage amplifyer or cooldown reducrion.
Combo builder are the main way to spend our main ressource and create our secoundary ressource.
Finishing abilities are used to spend the secoundary ressource but also require our main ressource, our damage is shifted into those(over 60% of our damage comes from finishing abilities).
Utility abilities are used to provide you mobility, healing or damage reduction. There are also additional utility that comes from aoe stealth and stuns.

Talents in Detail:

[Master of Subtlety]

Provides additional damage during Shadowdance/Vnaish/Stealth. This talent is the best choice in combinations with high Shadowdance uptimes.

[Weaponmaster]

Provides additional damage from a random proc. It is about equal or slightly ahead depending on talent choices. Multi Target or AoE situations bring this talent ahead. It is not adviced to use it with DFA due to a Bug.

[Gloomblade]

Is a situational Talent choice. It synergises well with Insignia of Ravenholdt and works well on fights where you attack the boss from the front the majority of the time.

[Nightstalker]

Provides additional damage during Shadowdance/Vanish/Stealth. It become a strong choice in AoE situations and is about equal to Shadow Focus in singe target situations.

[Subterfuge]

Provides one additional secound of Shadowdance. The changes made the talent in general less powerfull but it synergises weill with theShadow Satyr's Walk Legendary.

[Shadow Focus]

Provides less energy costs for abilities. This lowers the energy trashhold you can Dance with and reliably fit in multiple abilities.

[Deeper Stratagem]

Is the strongest talent of this row. It provides 5% additional damage on Finisher, additional damage from the linear CP scaling if you finish at 6 CP ontop of a stronger Finality.

[Anticipation]

Is the best talent if you want to prevent ccombo point overcaping. It makes it relatively easy to manage Combo Points however its behind Deeper Strategem due to the additional damage Scaling.

[Vigor]

Provides you a higher energy pool and slightly srtronger Energy regeneration. Its adviced to use this talent if you are new to the spec.

[Dark Shadow]

Shifts your damage in the Dance window. We usualy want higher burst damage, this meakes this options the go-to choice.

[Alacrity]

Provides us with haste but is relatively seen to the other talent choices weak.

[Enveloping Shadows]

Provides higher Shadowdance cdr and an additional charge. The Shadowdance uptime is significant higher providing higher benefits from soothing darkness. Is likely a good choice in aoe situations.

[Master of Shadows]

Provides additional energy during dance that allowes you to use abilities more frequently. Is likely a good choice in AoE situations.

[Marked for Death]

Is about equal on single target fights to Death from Above. Is potent in fights where you dont want to use Death from Above due to boss design or on fights where you are able to consistantly abuse the reset.

[Death from Above]

Is the go-to talent choice of this talent row. The strengh of this talent came mainly from the changes to Shadowdance that alowes us to use Shadowdance during the animation and get additional dmage to the Evi part of Death from Above while not losing ability casts during Shadowdance.
