Subtlety Guide

Equipment

This part of the Guide covers everything related to your gear.

Gearing Content and System:

The main sources of gear are raids. However, Legion introduced additional ways to get loot. One of the big sources is Mythic Plus, a scaling dungeon system with rewards relative to the difficulty level of the dungeon. Another source is the War/Titanforged system that enables any gear that drops to become a lot more powerful. Professions are also an additional source that helps you on lower gear levels but are limited.

Mythic Plus chest:

If you complete a Mythic Plus dungeon of at least a level 10 or higher, you receive a 935 or more, depending on Titanforging item level piece of gear in the weekly loot chest found in your class order hall resets during weekly reset.

Enchants, Consumables and Gems:

Enchants, consumables and gems give your character additional small power gains.
You can enchant your Back, Neck and both rings permanently with either stats or additional proc effects.
Consumables are mostly one-time-use effects that don't persist through death, with the exception of flasks.
Gem slots are procs from the Titanforging/Bonus Stat system, or exist naturally on legendary rings/necks, as well as crafted legendaries.

Healing:   [Aged Health Potion]

Stealth:  [Draenic Invisibility Potion] - usually not used as rogue because it shares cooldown with combat potions like  [Potion of Prolonged Power]

Enchants:

Enchants can vary depending on your gear and the scenario.
The general aim is to get Mastery > Versatility > Critical Strike > Haste
It is recommended to run a simulation to determine the best enchants for your gear.

Neck:   [Mark of the Trained Soldier]
Back:   [Binding of Agility]

Relics

Relics offer optimization to your artifact weapon and bring item level and one (two with 7.3) additional point in one of the minor artifact traits.
The power levels of certain traits depends on a lot of things including your gear, the boss, your Netherlight Crucible traits, and your guild's strategy.
The best way to evaluate relics is to run a simulation.

Trait Recommendation
[Energetic Stabbing] One of the strongest pure single-target traits (loses a lot of value as soon as any AoE is introduced to the fight).
[Weak Point] Extremely potent with  [Insignia of Ravenholdt]  also good without [Insignia of Ravenholdt] on single-target fights.
[Soul Shadows] Very strong with  [Denial of the Half-Giants] on single-target and multi-target fights.
[Gutripper] Strong trait single-target and multi-target fights.

[Fortune's Bite] Worst DPS trait. Gets a bit stronger if you select  [Anticipation].


Netherlight crucible:

The Netherlight Crucible is a relic customization tool introduced in 7.3 that has 3 tiers. Tier one is always a flat item level increase for your relics, up to +15 per. Tier two has 1 of 6  random light and shadow traits you need to choose between.Tier three has 3   minor traits that can't be the same as the initial minor trait. You can only choose between 2 of them depending on what Tier 2 choice you made.
You need to have 60/63/66 points in your artifact weapon (8/11/14 points in   [Concordance of the Legionfall] )to unlock the Tier 2 traits, and 69/72/75 points in your artifact weapon to unlock the Tier 3 traits.

Legendaries

The strength of a legendary item depends on your gear, your available replacements for legendaries, as well as the specific situation.
The so-called "Best in Slot" legendary combination can therefore be weaker for your current situation due to equipped relics and/or your equipped gear.
Simulations can be used to get a basic knowledge of each legendary's strength relative to each other.
The best way to find the best legendary combination is to run a simulation.
That being said, typically the current strongest combination for single-target fights is  [Mantle of the Master Assassin] and  [The First of the Dead].

Legendary
[Denial of the Half-Giants] Strong legendary only in combination with  [Convergence of Fates]
[Mantle of the Master Assassin] Best legendary for the Spec that turns  [Vanish] into an strong dps cooldown..
[Soul of the Shadowblade] Strong choice in AoE fights. Also makes the spec play a bit more smoothly.
[Kil'jaeden's Burning Wish] Decent trinket for mythic plus or on fights that need burst aoe.
[Shadow Satyr's Walk] Strong Single target choice that requires some practice for optimal use.
[The Dreadlord's Deceit] Strong in certain burst AoE situations because we can line it up with our damage-increasing cooldowns.
[Cinidaria, the Symbiote] An underrated legendary that is strong due to our high initial burst.
[The First of the Dead] Our strongest single target legendary.
[Insignia of Ravenholdt] Decent single target legendary that also works well with  [Gloomblade].

Gearing up and attribute distribution

As you gear up, you will start to optimize your gear around your secondary attributes as much as possible while still using as many of your high item level pieces as possible.
Certain secondary attributes become stronger if you have low amounts of them. The best way to determine the impact each stat will make based on your current gear is to run a simulation.



Trinkets:

Currently, the strongest trinket  is  [Specter of Betrayal]. It is used alongside various other trinkets.
[Convergence of Fates] loses value if you aren't using   [Denial of the Half-Giants] , and as such usually gets replaced with higher item level Tomb of Sargeras trinkets, such as [Engine of Eradication].
If you use 2 trinkets with an on-use effect, the use of one will trigger a 10 second cooldown for the other one.
In this case, the rotation will need to be adjusted to account for the cooldown imposed on the second trinket.
The best way to evaluate Trinkets is to run a simulations but you can look at the graphs provided by  Ravenholdt TC or  Bloodmallet to get an overview.
